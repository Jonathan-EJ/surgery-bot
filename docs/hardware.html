Gripper Design

Our gripper design improved along with the development of the coding for Baxter to function completely.

Prototype 1

Sponges wrapped with tape can hold the needle without falling, but has some slippage.

Prototype 2

Adding cardboard on the sponge to lock in the needle can hold it without falling and slippage but is not durable. It loses its stability overtime

Final Prototype

Using hot glue to create a series of horizontal tracks allow it to lock in the needle to prevent falling and slippage. This prototype is also more stable and durable.

Patient Design

Our patient design improved along with the development of the coding for Baxter to function completely.

Prototype 1

Yarn fabric is stuffed with cotton to elevate the wound, but cotton is moved when the needle is inserted making the wound to have irregular shape overtime.

Prototype 2

Using needles and strings to hold up the wound, but the structure is very fragile.

Final Prototype

Using cardboard to hold up the wound and hollowed the middle portion for the needle to go through. The structure is durable and consistent.
